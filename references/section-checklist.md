# Section Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing the manuscript.

## Abstract
- state the problem and why it matters
- identify the proposed method or framing clearly
- summarize the main evidence without overclaiming
- end with the actual contribution, not generic impact language

## Introduction
- define the gap precisely
- explain why current methods are insufficient for the target setting
- state contributions as factual capabilities supported by the paper

## Method
- define inputs, outputs, and the main modeling idea
- surface important assumptions instead of hiding them in notation
- keep the description reproducible at a high level even if full implementation details are external

## Results
- map each subsection to a claim
- interpret metrics instead of listing them mechanically
- mention limitations or non-wins when they matter to a fair reading

## Discussion
- explain what the results mean within realistic scope
- highlight limitations and open questions
- avoid unsupported practical promises

## Captions And Assets
- make captions self-contained
- state what each panel shows and how to read the metric direction when needed
- never mention statistics that are absent from the source material
